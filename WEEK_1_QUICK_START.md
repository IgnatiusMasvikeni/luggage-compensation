## Interview Guide Template

Save as `./research/INTERVIEW_GUIDE.md`

```markdown
# Customer Interview Guide

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes  
**Medium:** Zoom  
**Recording:** Ask permission, record (optional, for accuracy)

## Opener (2 min)
"Thanks for taking time to chat. I'm building a tool to help people get fair compensation for lost luggage. I'd love to hear about your experience."

## Problem Exploration (10 min)
1. "Tell me about the worst luggage experience you've had."
   - Listen for: What happened, how they felt, what went wrong
   
2. "What happened after? How did you try to resolve it?"
   - Listen for: Process they went through, frustrations, timeline

3. "What would have made this easier?"
   - Listen for: Gaps, pain points, what they needed

## Solution Validation (10 min)
4. "If there was a tool that helped you write a professional claim letter and understand your rights, would that help?"
   - Listen for: Yes/no, enthusiasm level, conditions

5. "What would a tool like this need to do for you to actually use it?"
   - Listen for: Specific features, must-haves, deal-breakers

## Willingness to Pay (5 min)
6. "Would you pay for this tool? If so, how much?"
   - Listen for: Dollar amount, price sensitivity, "I'd pay because..."

7. "How often do you have luggage issues?"
   - Listen for: Annual frequency, seasonal patterns

## Closing (5 min)
8. "Is there anything else about luggage compensation that frustrates you?"
   - Listen for: Unspoken needs, secondary problems

9. "Would you be interested in trying an early version when it's ready?"
   - Listen for: Willingness to be beta user

10. "Can I follow up with you in April when we have something to show?"
    - Listen for: Permission for future contact

## Notes for Interviewer
- Don't sell, listen
- Write down exact quotes
- Ask "why?" when you hear something interesting
- Don't interrupt
- Be authentic ("I'm trying to build something useful")
```

## Feeling Stuck?

**Question:** I don't know anyone to interview  
**Answer:** Start with Reddit/Twitter. Search "luggage" and reply to posts. People are usually happy to chat if you're genuine.

**Question:** How do I actually schedule a Zoom?  
**Answer:** Use Calendly (free). It syncs with your Google Calendar and sends Zoom links automatically.

**Question:** What if people say "no"?  
**Answer:** That's data! It's actually valuable to know. Keep going until you find 10 who will chat.

**Question:** What if I realize this is a bad idea?  
**Answer:** Perfect! That's the whole point of Phase 1. Better to learn now than build for 12 weeks.
